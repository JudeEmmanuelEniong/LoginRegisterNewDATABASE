About the System

CCS Sit-in Monitoring System

📌 Overview

The CCS Sit-in Monitoring System is a web-based application developed for the College of Computer Studies at the University of Cebu. It is designed to efficiently manage and monitor student sit-in sessions in the computer laboratories.

🎯 Purpose

The system aims to replace manual logbook recording with a digital solution that tracks student attendance, manages laboratory usage, and provides administrators with real-time data and reports on sit-in activities.

⚙️ Key Features

  • Student registration and login system
  • Real-time sit-in session monitoring
  • Laboratory usage tracking per programming language
  • Admin dashboard with statistics and charts
  • Announcement posting for students
  • Sit-in session reports and feedback management
  • Lab reservation system

📋 Laboratory Rules & Regulations
University of Cebu
COLLEGE OF INFORMATION & COMPUTER STUDIES
LABORATORY RULES AND REGULATIONS

To avoid embarrassment and maintain camaraderie with your friends and superiors at our laboratories, please observe the following:

  1. Maintain silence, proper decorum, and discipline inside the laboratory. Mobile phones, walkmans and other personal pieces of equipment must be switched off.
  2. Games are not allowed inside the lab. This includes computer-related games, card games and other games that may disturb the operation of the lab.
  3. Surfing the Internet is allowed only with the permission of the instructor. Downloading and installing of software are strictly prohibited.
  4. Getting access to other websites not related to the course is strictly prohibited.
  5. Deleting computer files and changing the computer settings are not allowed.
  6. Observe computer time. All requests must be done 5 minutes before time.
  7. No food and drinks allowed inside the laboratory.
  8. Students are held responsible for any damage to computer equipment caused by their negligence.
  9. Laboratory entrance is limited to students with proper laboratory attire.
  10. Only authorized persons are allowed to operate and handle laboratory equipment.
